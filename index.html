Stanley Varnhagen

Evaluator

Bio: Stanley Varnhagen has a Ph.D. in Educational Psychology from the University of California, Santa Barbara. He is in the Sociology Department, and is affiliated with the Masters of Arts in Communications and Technology (MACT), which is with Media and Technology Studies (MTS), within the in the Faculty of Arts. His specialty is in evaluation. His professional interests include program, utilization-focused, participatory, and community-based evaluation; education; use of innovative methods to facilitate learning; and research ethics. He also is currently an Associate Chair of two University of Alberta’s Research Ethics Boards (REB 1 & 2).

Education

  • Ph.D. University of California, Santa Barbara, 1984 Educational Psychology “Use of feedback on microcomputers to teach spelling in elementary school”

  • M.A. University of California, Santa Barbara, 1980 Educational Psychology “Instructional aids in computer assisted instruction for remedial fractions in junior high school"

  • B.A. Kenyon College, 1977 Psychology
